Krak-a-what-a?

 

Did you know that Indonesia has more volcanoes than any other country? One of its historically nasty eruptions occurred from a volcano known as Krakatau or you may have heard it referred to as Krakatoa. It erupted in 1883 and was so loud it was probably heard in the Maldives! If you check your atlas you'll see that's a long way so either the Maldivians have really good hearing or it really was very loud!

During the eruption most of the island of Krakatau collapsed into the underlying magma chamber, forming a giant crater or hole known as a 'caldera'. It sent flying tens' of cubic kilometres of material (don't forget one cubic kilometre = 1 km x 1 km x 1 km = pretty big). It also caused large tsunamis, or giant waves, as the volcano collapsed a bit like what happens when you plonk into the bath! The eruption was so big that it sadly killed more than 36,000 people.

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
