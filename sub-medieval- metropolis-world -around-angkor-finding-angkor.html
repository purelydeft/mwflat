Where did they go?

Want to see it? You'll find the site of Angkor north of the great lake in the middle of Cambodia called Tonle Sap. The Khmer kings liked this location because it was in a good position between the lake and the Kulen hills. Not only was the land fertile, but water ran down from the hills to the lake, making it easier to redirect water through Angkor's canals into its network of rice fields and reservoirs.

Zooming in to view the site of Angkor from satellite images today, you see large rectangular outlines flanking smaller square marks on the landscape. The most noticeable is the huge reservoir of the West Baray (7.8 long and 2.1 kilometres wide) built almost 1000 years ago. On the other side, you can make out the East Baray. The square imprints in the middle covered in forest are the walls and moats of Angkor Thom and Angkor Wat.
