Living with volcanoes

Some volcanic eruptions on our Earth have been bigger and far more destructive than many others.

Before we look at these, it's important to know how volcanoes are measured and compared. Volcanologists use the Volcanic Explosivity Index or VEI scale. It measures the amount (volume) of erupted products, the height of the eruption cloud above the volcano and how long the eruption takes.

The scale is logarithmic. This means each time you go up a level, the eruption is ten times greater! So a VEI 6 eruption like Mt Pinatubo, in the Philippines in 1991, was ten times BIGGER than a VEI 5 eruption like the Mt St Helens eruption in the United States of America in 1980!
