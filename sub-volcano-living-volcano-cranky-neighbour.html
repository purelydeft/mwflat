A cranky neighbour

 

What would it be like to live next door to a volcano? You could ask a Sicilian or Roman in Italy or a resident living around Lake Taupo in New Zealand! Many people worldwide live in, on, or around volcanoes. It can be dangerous because some of these volcanoes are active and volcanoes don't always erupt when or how you might expect.

However, volcanic deposits from eruptions can become wonderful, fertile soils over time. That is good for agriculture and the reason why people often choose to live or work in the shadow of an active, fiery giant.

TImagine living in the town of Kagoshima, Japan. You would need to pack your umbrella when you go outside, not for rain but for volcanic ash that might start falling! The town sits next to the gigantic Aira caldera volcano, which has an active volcano inside it which often erupts, called Sakurajima.

We are all made of atoms, and the different kinds of atoms are called elements. Oxygen, hydrogen, carbon, iron and gold are all elements.

In our daily lives, elements remain unchanged. You cannot easily turn hydrogen into helium or carbon into oxygen.

But in the super hot and super dense core of a star, hydrogen atoms are fused together to become helium atoms. This is nuclear fusion, and this process powers all stars including our Sun.
