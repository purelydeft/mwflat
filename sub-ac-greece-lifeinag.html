Life in Ancient Greece

 

Histories of the Ancient Greeks usually focus on the wars they fought - often with each other! However, the Greeks did more than just go to war. They also developed and enjoyed a lively society, and were proud of this fact. As the Greek poet Archilochus said in the 7th century BCE, he would happily abandon his shield to save his life: "oh well," he said, "I can always buy a new one!"

The Athenians were proud of the way they spent their time when not at war. They went to the gym! The palaestra, was their form of gym, where they exercised and socialised. The Greeks also organised elaborate festivals, played sports, debated the meaning of life, and went to the theatre!
