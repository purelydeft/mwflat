You know, of course, that ancient civilisations did not have modern technologies like computers, TVs, and movie theatres. They had their own ways to have fun - playing music, attending live theatre, and by telling stories...

Ancient Greek stories were told by singing verses of poetry. The singers of the stories were very skilful - they could remember thousands of verses without any written records or notes!

Stories were told to educate children, record histories and legends, to inspire and bind communities together, and just to entertain.

The most famous Greek poet and story teller was Homer. Homer is credited with writing the Iliad and the Odyssey, but historians think he may not have composed all of them by himself... each of them contain more than 13,000 verses!

Even today, nearly 3,000 years later, they're pretty cool stories... !

Homer is perhaps the most important ancient Greek poet. He lived and composed his verses in the first half of the eight century BCE. He was probably a travelling bard from ancient Chios or Smyrna who offered his services for a fee. He may also have been a member of a guild of singers. He wrote the Iliad and the Odyssey, famous stories that have inspired poets, artists, and storytellers ever since... including modern movie makers!
