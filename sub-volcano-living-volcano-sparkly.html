The good, the bad and the sparkly

 

There are many good things about volcanoes. They can produce energy, known as geothermal energy. They can also create new land for us, can positively change our atmosphere, produce valuable mineral deposits, and provide fertile soils for plants to grow! But volcanic eruptions big or small do have the potential to cause trouble too, not just for us but our animals, homes, communities and the environment.

Have you ever wondered where diamonds come from? Diamonds are made in the Earth's mantle about 160 kilometres below your feet under great pressure and temperatures. Once made they sit and wait, sometimes for a very long time, until they are picked up and taken to the surface by magma, erupting from special types of volcanoes known as kimberlites and lamproites. So next time you see a diamond, remember it's been brought to you by a volcano!

We are all made of atoms, and the different kinds of atoms are called elements. Oxygen, hydrogen, carbon, iron and gold are all elements.

In our daily lives, elements remain unchanged. You cannot easily turn hydrogen into helium or carbon into oxygen.

But in the super hot and super dense core of a star, hydrogen atoms are fused together to become helium atoms. This is nuclear fusion, and this process powers all stars including our Sun.
