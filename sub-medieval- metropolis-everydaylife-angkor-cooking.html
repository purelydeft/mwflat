Cooking and Cuisine

 

If you lived in Angkor, you would eat almost the same food as you would in Cambodia today. They had a healthy diet of mainly rice and fish. Rice-growing was really important, but there were also heaps of fish near the city of Angkor and this was also essential in sustaining the people. They ate fish in different ways fresh, dried, salted or smoked, or made into a fermented strong-smelling paste called prahok.

Of course, the Angkorians enjoyed lots of other foods as well. They had sugar cane and palm sugar for sweeter flavours and mustard and tamarind could season sour tastes. There were all kinds of fruits, like jackfruit, mango and durian, and all kinds of different vegetables too.

urtles, snakes, snails and frogs were also on the menu, as well as roasted water bugs and crickets! Have you ever eaten any of these delicacies?

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
