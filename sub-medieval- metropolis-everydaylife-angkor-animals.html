Watch out for the Elephants!

 

The Angkorians used animals for work, transport and farming. You can tell that elephants were very important because they appear prominently in decorations carrying military commanders and princes into battle. Elephants were also used in royal processions, and if you were walking along the roads of Angkor you'd also need to keep out of the way of elephants ridden by high-ranking people! Elephants and water buffaloes also helped to pull or lift heavy stone and timber in temple construction.

The Angkorian's used cattle to pull carts (but not to eat!), and they sometimes rode ponies. As you wandered through the markets and villages you'd also see chickens, pigs, ducks and goats. The people of Angkor never lived far from the water, where there were plenty of fish, turtles and waterbirds to catch, or the forest, where they hunted deer and wild boar, so wild game was always available. But of course people had to be careful not to be 'on the menu' themselves for tigers, leopards or crocodiles!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
