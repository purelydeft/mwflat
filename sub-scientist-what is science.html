What is Science?

 

Scientists are like detectives who find out how the world works. They answer questions that nobody knows the answer to. You can't Google the answer to the questions that scientists ask! Scientists need to work out the answers for themselves.

Like a detective, scientists gather evidence to help them answer the questions they are working on. Then they make deductions using this evidence, to find the answers to their questions. This is called the "scientific method". Scientists have to be able to give a logical reason for the answers they give. They can't just make up the answer, they have to justify it using their brains!

Scientists like working on really hard questions, which may take months or even years to answer! The questions that scientists answer are important and the answers can help make the world a better place. They can also be interesting questions. They can even be both important and interesting questions!
