Death of a volcano

Volcanoes might run out of puff and become extinct or die because their magma source has stopped or moved away. They might have been so explosive that they blew themselves apart! Some might come back to life, even after thousands of years like the Yellowstone caldera.

It's not easy to call 'time of death' on a volcano. You can get a fairly good idea if you know when it last erupted, how often it has erupted and if there are signs on the surface or underground that magma is still there or moving. A dormant volcano is the same as an inactive volcano meaning the volcano hasn't erupted for a long time, sometimes thousands of years. But it is likely to erupt again. So don't be fooled if it's covered in grass and your family has had picnics on it for generations that doesn't mean it won't erupt in the future!
