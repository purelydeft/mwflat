The Big Tomato

 

As an example of the a scientific approach, let's ask a useful and interesting question. "How do I grow bigger tomatoes in my vegetable garden?" This is a great question!

Imagine you are the scientist. You will need to gather evidence. To do this, you might put 100 plants in the garden. You might grow 25 plants in the same way you have always done. For the next 25 plants, you might put twice as much water on them each week. For the third batch of 25 plants, you might put on extra compost. The fourth and final batch might have extra water and extra compost. After three months of doing this, you could harvest the tomatoes and weigh them to find out which method gave the biggest tomatoes.

If one of the new methods had made bigger tomatoes, you would have answered the question of how to grow bigger tomatoes! If none of the methods made any difference, then you could repeat the experiment by changing different factors until you came up with a way that made the tomatoes bigger.

We are all made of atoms, and the different kinds of atoms are called elements. Oxygen, hydrogen, carbon, iron and gold are all elements.

In our daily lives, elements remain unchanged. You cannot easily turn hydrogen into helium or carbon into oxygen.

But in the super hot and super dense core of a star, hydrogen atoms are fused together to become helium atoms. This is nuclear fusion, and this process powers all stars including our Sun.
