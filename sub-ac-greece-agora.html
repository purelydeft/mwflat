The Agora

 

The Agora was the market place of the ancient Greek city Athens. People would go there to buy fish, meat, fruit, cheese, pots and other goods. Slaves, often accompanied by the children of the family, were responsible for the daily shopping.

The Agora was also the place where people met to discuss politics and to debate ways of thinking about the world. Athenians were proud of their system of politics, which they called demokratia (democracy). Democracy in Athens meant people could vote about decisions in their society. But democracy in Athens was quite limited. Only some men could vote, and women were not allowed to vote at all!
