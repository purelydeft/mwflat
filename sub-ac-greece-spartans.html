Spartan Society and War

Sparta in the early days was a lively place where the arts flourished and poets such as Alcman wrote verses about beauty and love. However, the long wars which the Spartans had to fight to establish themselves in their homeland changed the character of their society. Sparta became xenophobic and strictly dedicated to the art of war.

The Spartans fought long wars against their local rivals, the Messenians and against other Greeks, mainly the Athenians and their allies. They also fought with the Athenians, against the Persians in the sixth century.

Spartan children left their homes and lived together with other children, learning to fight and even to steal. They were encouraged to steal in order to feed themselves, but if caught they should never admit it. This might seem strange in today's world, but the Spartans were teaching their children how to survive in a tough and brutal world.
