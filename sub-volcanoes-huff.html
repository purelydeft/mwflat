Huff and puff vs. flow

 

Some volcanic eruptions are very explosive, producing huge eruption clouds or 'columns' that can soar high into the sky. These eruptions can also form deadly and destructive pyroclastic flows that can bury houses, fields, parts of rivers anything in their path!

Other eruptions are less explosive and produce lava flows that ooze and glide over the landscape. These eruptions might look kind of harmless but they're not a good spot to toast marshmallows because lava temperatures can reach up to 1250°C so you might just toast off your hand and arm too!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
